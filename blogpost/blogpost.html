Schipperplein

At the heart of the entertainment centre lies a public square called Schipperplein. The garden is fringed by commercial functions and has an undulating relief. The small groups of trees, given time, will grow to provide shady canopies. A meandering stream, which will be frozen to form a skating rink in winter, writhes across the middle of the square.

One of the major square in Amsterdam

By Caroline Johansonn

Perhaps the most expansive of Amsterdam's squares, Museumplein (Museum Square) is appropriately named for the two major museums located on its vast lawn, in addition to the many other attractions near the square.

"The museum continues to put on exhibits and events in borrowed exhibition spaces"

The beauty of the square's landscape matches that of the museum architecture, which comprises the Van Gogh Museum -- one of the most popular museums in Amsterdam, devoted to the troubled artist, his brilliant oeuvre, and his contemporaries -- and the Stedelijk Museum, Amsterdam's museum of modern art, which is currently under extensive renovation. The stellar collection of the Rijksmuseum is nearby, as well as the headquarters of Coster Diamonds, who offer tours of their facility to diamond enthusiasts.
